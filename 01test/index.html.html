Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the knowledge, skills, and habits needed to use digital technologies and online spaces safely, responsibly, and respectfully.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law is the set of legal principles, regulations, and frameworks that govern the creation, use, management, and protection of digital technologies, data, and online activities.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a formal document that outlines the rules and guidelines users must follow when using an organization's computers, networks, or digital services.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's work, ideas, or expressions as your own without proper attribution.

It involves taking credit for another person's words or ideas, whether intentionally or accidentally, and is considered a serious offense in academic and professional settings due to its violation of ethical standards.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty, also known as academic misconduct or academic fraud, encompasses a range of behaviors that violate the principles of honesty, trust, fairness, and responsibility in educational institutions.

It is not limited to students; faculty, researchers, and other academic professionals can also engage in dishonest practices.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

AI-generated content is subject to copyright rules that assign ownership to the person arranging its creation, while compliance with data protection, ethical, and advertising standards is also required.
